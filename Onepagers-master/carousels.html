Why homepage carousels are bad for users

They might look dynamic, but they often hurt usability, hide important content, and frustrate your visitors. Let's explore why a simpler approach is more effective.

See the Evidence

First, what is a carousel?

A carousel (also called a slider or banner rotator) is that rotating image section at the top of many websites. It automatically cycles through different images, messages, or promotions every few seconds.

The Problems are Significant

From banner blindness to poor performance, carousels introduce a host of usability issues.

1. People Don't See Them

Banner blindness is real. Visitors automatically ignore anything that looks like an ad. Since carousels often resemble ads, people's brains filter them out completely.

2. The Timing is Always Wrong

Carousels either change slides before people finish reading (frustrating) or move so slowly that impatient visitors leave before seeing other content. There's no "perfect" speed.

What Users Actually Want

Hint: It's clarity and control, not cleverness and automation.

Clarity Over Cleverness

Visitors come to your website with a goal. They want to quickly understand what you offer and find what they need. A simple, clear message works better than a fancy rotating showcase.

Control Over Automation

People want to browse at their own pace, not have content forced on them by a timer. Give users control over their own journey.
